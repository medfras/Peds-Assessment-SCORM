# Priority Stack

High-acuity calls require ranking competing tasks. The safest priority is the one that addresses the most immediate life threat.

## Ranking Lens

- Airway and ventilation failure rise to the top.
- Oxygenation problems require immediate support.
- Poor perfusion or shock must be treated early.
- Time-sensitive medications or procedures come before secondary history.
- Transport and ALS intercept move up when definitive care is the treatment.
- Detailed history, comfort measures, and secondary assessment follow immediate threats.

## Common Traps

- Treating a checklist as more important than a failing airway or perfusion problem.
- Anchoring on the first complaint after new findings show deterioration.
- Delaying transport for information that can be gathered en route.
