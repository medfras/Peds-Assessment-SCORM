# Rule of Nines

Use the Rule of Nines as a fast field estimate of burned body surface area. It helps frame severity, transport priority, burn-center consultation, fluid-risk thinking, and special-area concern.

## Adult Field Estimate

- Head/neck: 9%
- Each arm: 9%
- Anterior torso: 18%
- Posterior torso: 18%
- Each leg: 18%
- Perineum/genital area: 1%

## Pediatric Caution

Children have proportionally larger heads and smaller legs than adults. The Rule of Nines is a field estimate, not a replacement for pediatric burn charts or local burn-center tools.

## High-Risk Implications

- Burns to face, airway, hands, feet, genitalia, major joints, or circumferential areas can be high significance even at smaller percentages.
- Prevent heat loss after stopping the burning process.
- Avoid broad ice application.
- Reassess airway, breathing, perfusion, pain, and mechanism-related trauma.
