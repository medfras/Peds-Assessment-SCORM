# Shock Spotter: Trauma

Trauma shock recognition combines mechanism, hemorrhage risk, pulse quality, skin, mentation, and blood pressure trend. Do not wait for hypotension to start thinking shock.

## Compensated Trauma Shock

- Tachycardia, pallor, cool skin, delayed cap refill, anxiety, weak peripheral pulses, significant mechanism, or suspected internal bleeding.
- Systolic blood pressure may remain normal.

## Decompensated Trauma Shock

- Hypotension, altered mentation, weak central pulses, absent peripheral pulses, mottling, respiratory fatigue, or obvious uncontrolled hemorrhage with collapse.

## Common Traps

- Underestimating internal bleeding because the scene has little visible blood.
- Missing shock in isolated femur, pelvis, abdominal, or thoracic trauma.
