# Developmental Red Flags

Pediatric developmental surveillance means knowing what is expected at each age — and knowing when a finding should raise concern versus require immediate evaluation.

## Three Categories

**Expected** — The milestone is appropriate for this age. No action beyond routine well-child surveillance.

**Concerning / Regression** — The child has lost a previously acquired skill (regression), OR the finding is outside the normal range in a clinically meaningful way. Refer promptly. Do not "wait and see" when regression is present — it is never explained by normal developmental variation.

**Needs Further Assessment** — The finding sits at the edge of the normal age window, or context could change its significance. Document and follow up at the next well-child visit or sooner if other concerns are present.

## Key Rules

**Regression is always concerning.** Loss of previously acquired language, motor, or self-care skills is a red flag regardless of the apparent trigger. A new sibling, a move, or a stressor may be present, but regression still requires pediatric follow-up.

**Age windows overlap.** A milestone missed at the early edge of a window may resolve. Missed at the late edge, it is a red flag. Know both ends of the window, not just the midpoint.

**Screen proactively.** AAP developmental surveillance is expected at every well-child visit using validated tools (M-CHAT-R/F, ASQ, PEDS). EMS providers do not screen formally, but they observe.

**In EMS:** Developmental concerns affect:
- Transport destination decisions (pediatric-capable facility vs. community ED)
- Parental communication and rapport
- Mandatory reporting obligations when developmental delay accompanies an injury or neglect pattern

## Milestone Anchors

| Age | Gross Motor | Language | Social |
|-----|-------------|----------|--------|
| 2 months | Brief prone head lift | Coos | Social smile |
| 4 months | Rolls tummy-to-back | Laughs | Tracks faces |
| 6 months | Sits with support | Babbles | Reaches for objects |
| 9 months | Pulls to stand | Mama/dada (non-specific) | Waves, stranger anxiety |
| 12 months | Walks with support | 1 word | Points to request |
| 18 months | Runs (falls often) | 10+ words | Parallel play |
| 2 years | Runs well | 2-word phrases | Shows empathy |
| 3 years | Pedals tricycle | 3–4 word sentences | Cooperative play |
| 4 years | Hops on one foot | Complex sentences | Rule-based games |
| 5 years | Skips | Full conversation | Peer friendships |
