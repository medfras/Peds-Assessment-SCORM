# Sound Check: Breath Sounds

Sound Check trains the same field loop used in respiratory assessment: listen carefully, name the sound, then connect it to the next clinical action.

## Listen For

- Clear: expected air movement without added sounds. Distress can still come from upper airway, perfusion, anxiety, metabolic causes, or early deterioration.
- Crackles: discontinuous popping or bubbling, often tied to fluid, pneumonia, or alveolar disease.
- Wheezes: continuous musical sounds from narrowed lower airways.
- Rhonchi: low, snoring or gurgling central-airway sounds that may change with coughing or suction.
- Pleural friction rub: harsh, leathery sound from inflamed pleural surfaces.

## Field Actions

- Match the sound to the airway level before choosing treatment.
- Reassess after interventions; a changing sound may matter more than the first label.
- Treat the patient presentation, not the audio clip alone.

## Common Traps

- Calling all noisy breathing wheezing and missing upper-airway obstruction.
- Assuming clear lung sounds rule out respiratory danger.
- Forgetting that rhonchi may improve with cough, positioning, or suction.
