# Temp Check

Temperature emergencies are treatment-priority problems, not hot/cold sorting drills. Use exposure, mental status, skin findings, temperature trend, and infection risk to choose the safest field action.

## Priority Patterns

- Heat stroke: altered mental status with heat exposure or exertion. Rapid cooling and transport are priorities.
- Heat exhaustion: weakness, sweating, nausea, or dizziness without altered mental status. Remove from heat, cool, hydrate if safe, and reassess.
- Severe hypothermia: altered mental status, bradycardia, stopped shivering, or dysrhythmia risk. Handle gently and prevent further heat loss.
- Fever/sepsis: fever plus altered mental status, poor perfusion, petechiae, or respiratory distress requires sepsis screening and rapid transport.

## Common Traps

- Treating a confused hot patient as simple dehydration.
- Rough handling of severe hypothermia.
- Missing sepsis because the problem is framed as “just a fever.”
