# Differential Dash: AMS

Differential Dash trains rapid altered-mental-status pattern recognition. The goal is not to memorize a list; it is to connect observable findings to the reversible threat they suggest.

## First-Pass AMS Priorities

- Check glucose early when diaphoresis, tremor, diabetes history, seizure, or unexplained confusion appears.
- Correct oxygenation and ventilation problems before spending time on detailed history.
- Treat perfusion failure, temperature extremes, toxins, trauma, seizure, stroke, and infection as time-sensitive until ruled out.

## Common Traps

- Calling AMS psychiatric before checking glucose, oxygenation, trauma, and toxidromes.
- Treating pinpoint pupils as opioids only; toxins and organophosphates can also cause miosis.
- Missing infection or sepsis because the patient is young or initially compensating.
