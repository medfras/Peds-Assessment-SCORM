# MOI Mapper

Mechanism of injury is a prediction tool. It tells you which hidden injuries deserve extra attention before they announce themselves.

## What To Map

- Energy transfer: fall height, speed, compression, blast, penetration, rollover, ejection, or crush.
- Body region: head/neck, chest, abdomen, pelvis, extremity, or whole-body.
- Field implication: hidden injury suspicion, spinal motion consideration, hemorrhage risk, transport priority, or focused reassessment.

## Common Traps

- Treating normal first vitals as proof that a high-risk mechanism is benign.
- Missing abdominal injury after handlebar or lap-belt mechanisms.
- Forgetting that pediatric patients may compensate until they suddenly do not.
