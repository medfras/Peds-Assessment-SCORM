# Shock Spotter: Medical

Medical shock recognition is about spotting compensation before collapse. In pediatrics especially, blood pressure can look acceptable while perfusion and mentation are already deteriorating.

## Compensated Shock

- Tachycardia, anxiety, pallor, cool skin, delayed capillary refill, weak peripheral pulses, or narrow pulse pressure.
- Systolic blood pressure may still be normal.
- Treat the trend and perfusion picture, not one isolated number.

## Decompensated Shock

- Hypotension, lethargy, weak or absent peripheral pulses, poor skin signs, worsening respiratory effort, or falling mental status.
- Compensation is failing; transport and protocol-directed support become more urgent.

## Common Traps

- Waiting for hypotension before recognizing shock.
- Calling tachycardia "just fever" without checking perfusion and mental status.
- Missing that respiratory failure, sepsis, dehydration, and anaphylaxis can all produce shock patterns.
