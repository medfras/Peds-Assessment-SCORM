# PAT & TICLS

![Pediatric Assessment Triangle](/static/img/minigames/pat/pat-triangle.png)

The PAT is a rapid, hands-off doorway assessment used to form a general impression of a pediatric patient's stability before you touch them. It takes under 30 seconds and gives you an immediate sense of whether a child is sick or not sick.

## TICLS - Appearance Mnemonic

- **T - Tone:** Is the child moving, resisting, or floppy?
- **I - Interactivity:** Are they alert and engaged with their environment?
- **C - Consolability:** Can a parent calm them? Inconsolable crying is a red flag.
- **L - Look / Gaze:** Do they track faces? Fixed or glassy gaze is abnormal.
- **S - Speech / Cry:** Age-appropriate vocalization? Weak cry or no words is concerning.

## Three Components

- **Appearance:** TICLS - mental status and muscle tone.
- **Breathing:** Sounds, position, visible effort.
- **Circulation:** Skin color and perfusion signs.
