# Differential Dash: Respiratory

Respiratory distress is not one protocol. The pattern matters: upper airway, lower airway, alveolar/fluid, infection, allergic exposure, toxin, trauma, or fatigue.

## First-Pass Respiratory Sorting

- Upper airway: stridor, barking cough, drooling, tripod positioning, sudden choking, or muffled voice.
- Lower airway: wheezes, prolonged expiration, asthma history, allergen exposure, or bronchodilator response.
- Alveolar/fluid/infection: crackles, fever, focal lung findings, edema, hypoxia, or fatigue.

## Common Traps

- Treating stridor as wheezing.
- Missing impending respiratory failure because wheezing becomes quieter.
- Ignoring exposure history in anaphylaxis, smoke inhalation, or toxic inhalation.
