# History Maker

Round 1 matches pediatric history clues to OPQRST and SAMPLE categories. Round 2 asks you to build the actual field question from phrase chunks. The reference card is earned after passing both rounds.

## OPQRST

- Onset: when it started and what the patient was doing.
- Provocation/Palliation: what makes it better or worse.
- Quality: what it feels like in the patient's own words.
- Radiation/Region: where it is and whether it moves.
- Severity: how bad it is on an age-appropriate scale.
- Time: how symptoms have changed over time.

## SAMPLE

- Signs/Symptoms: what the patient is experiencing right now, including objective findings and subjective complaints.
- Allergies: known medication, food, or environmental allergies and the reaction type when known.
- Medications: prescribed, over-the-counter, and rescue medications, including inhalers, epinephrine auto-injectors, and supplements.
- Past Medical History: significant illness, surgeries, hospitalizations, chronic conditions, and relevant birth or developmental history.
- Last Oral Intake: when and what the patient last ate or drank, especially before sedation, vomiting, seizure, or abdominal complaints.
- Events/Environment: what happened before the call, what changed, who witnessed it, and what exposures or mechanisms may matter.
