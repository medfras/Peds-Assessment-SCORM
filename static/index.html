    Practice applying the Pediatric Assessment Triangle (PAT) at doorway speed. You'll be shown clinical cues — swipe right for Sick or left for Not Sick before the 30-second timer runs out.

    Only doorway observations count — no hands-on data. Use the PAT to make your call fast.

    PAT & TICLS

    The PAT is a rapid, hands-off doorway assessment used to form a general impression of a pediatric patient's stability before you touch them. It takes under 30 seconds and gives you an immediate sense of whether a child is sick or not sick.

    TICLS — Appearance Mnemonic

    T
    Tone — Is the child moving, resisting, or floppy?
    I
    Interactivity — Are they alert and engaged with their environment?
    C
    Consolability — Can a parent calm them? Inconsolable crying is a red flag.
    L
    Look / Gaze — Do they track faces? Fixed or glassy gaze is abnormal.
    S
    Speech / Cry — Age-appropriate vocalization? Weak cry or no words is concerning.

    Appearance

    TICLS — mental status & muscle tone

    Breathing

    Sounds, position, visible effort

    Circulation

    Skin color & perfusion signs
